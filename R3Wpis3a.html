3D printing

3D printing is one of the latest technological developments that has been gaining popularity in recent years. Thanks to it, we can create items with three-dimensional models based on graphic files. 3D printing is gaining importance in various fields of life, from biomedicine and architecture to the aerospace industry.


A bit of history and how it works.

3D printing technology was created in the 1980s, but only in recent years has it become more accessible. The most popular ways to create objects in 3D printing are FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) and SLA (Stereolithography). With FDM, warm plastic is extruded from a nozzle and then builds up the item layer by layer. In contrast, in the case of SLA, this UV light is used to harden the plastic liquid and create layers that pass through it.


Usage

3D printing is gaining increasing use in industry, where it is currently used to produce prototypes, tools and spare parts. It is more efficient than traditional production methods, because in the case of 3D printing, there is no need to use special molds and tools.

3D printing is also used in medicine and biomedicine. Thanks to it, you can print anatomical models, dental crowns, implants and dentures for the needs of patients. This can increase the chances of successful surgery and reduce the risk of complications.

Another area where 3D printing is increasingly used is architecture and spatial design. Architects can use this technology to create models and mock-ups that allow them to accurately design spaces and see what they will look like in reality.


Summary

To sum up, 3D printing is a technology that is gaining popularity in various areas of life. It can bring many benefits in manufacturing, medicine, architecture and other fields. The ability to create objects with three-dimensional models allows for greater precision, sensitivity and saving time and money.
