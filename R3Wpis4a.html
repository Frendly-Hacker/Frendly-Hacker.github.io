Autonomous vehicles

Autonomous vehicles, i.e. cars without a driver, are a topic that is being discussed more and more these days. For years, scientists and representatives of the automotive industry have been working on the development of this technology, which has a chance to completely change the approach to driving on the roads.

On the one hand, autonomous vehicles have a chance to significantly improve transport and road safety. These cars are equipped with numerous sensors that allow for precise location determination, identification of obstacles on the road, monitoring of traffic around us, etc. Thanks to this, there is a chance for errors related to human error (e.g. too small distance between vehicles, not seeing pedestrians), which unfortunately very often end in tragic accidents, will be significantly reduced.

On the other hand, there are people's concerns about the use of such vehicles. Can you really trust a driverless car? Can anyone trust a technology that decides about acceleration, braking and direction without human intervention? These are questions to which we do not know the answers yet, but over time, along with the development of this technology, it will be possible to change the social approach to autonomous vehicles.

However, in order for autonomous vehicles to be able to move safely on the roads, they need appropriate regulations. Already in 2019, Poland introduced regulations specifying the conditions for testing autonomous vehicles on public roads. It is worth noting, however, that we still have a lot to do to create adequate regulations related to these vehicles.

Autonomous vehicles are certainly one of the most important scientific endeavors of our century. They are already available on a massive scale, which translates into demand on the market and lowers the estimated production costs. There is no doubt that they are the future of the automotive industry - beneficial for the environment and transport, bringing people closer!
